Work Process

Risk Identification

Identifying the types of financial risks that could impact an organization, such as market risk (e.g., stock price fluctuations), credit risk (e.g., default on loans), liquidity risk, operational risk, and interest rate risk.

Risk Assessment & Measurement

Assessing the likelihood and potential impact of identified risks. This often involves quantitative techniques like Value at Risk (VaR), stress testing, and scenario analysis to measure potential losses and financial exposure

Risk Mitigation & Management

Developing strategies to manage or reduce financial risks. This could include diversification, hedging (e.g., using options or derivatives), setting risk limits, or purchasing insurance to mitigate the potential financial impact.

Risk Monitoring & Reporting

Continuously monitoring financial risks and the effectiveness of risk management strategies. This involves regular reporting to stakeholders, tracking risk indicators, and adjusting strategies as market conditions, financial health, or regulatory environments change.
